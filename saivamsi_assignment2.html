PART 1 - Website Analysis

Step 1: Input and Output Analysis

Input (Action) Output (Result) Information Types Identified
Entering "Vietnam" in the search bar. A list of historical articles matching the keyword. Headline, Article Snippet, Date, Category Tags
Scrolling to the "Archives" section on the sidebar. A list of months and years of published content. Month/Year Labels, Article Counts (e.g., 67)
Clicking on "October 2021" in the Archives. A grid view of all stories from that specific month. Image Thumbnails, Authors, Comment Counts, Read More links
Clicking a specific article headline. The full article content page. Body Text, High-Resolution Images, Photo Credits

Step 2 & 3: Heuristic Summary

Evaluation Topic: Armed Conflict (Campus perspectives on war).

Top 2 Strengths

  • Consistency and Standards: The site uses a traditional newspaper layout which makes navigation predictable for researchers.
  • Aesthetic and Minimalist Design: The clean use of white space ensures that the primary source images and text are the main focus.

Top 2 Weaknesses

  • Efficiency of Use: There is no way to jump to a specific year without scrolling through a long list, which slows down historical research.
  • Visibility of System Status: The site does not clearly show how many total results were found after a search is performed.

PART 2 - Comparison and Suggestions

My analysis and the usability test confirmed that while the website is visually appealing, it lacks the advanced filtering needed for deep historical research. My participant noted that finding a specific date was the most difficult task.

Suggestions for Improvement: Add a "Filter by Year" dropdown menu and a "Sort by Relevance" feature to the search results page.
