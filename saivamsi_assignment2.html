Step 1: Input and Output Analysis

Step 2 & 3: Heuristic Summary

Evaluation Topic: Sports Reporting and Historical Information Retrieval.

Top 2 Strengths

  • Aesthetic and Minimalist Design: The sports section uses high-quality action photos that clearly identify the subject (e.g., Lacrosse or Softball) before reading the text.
  • Consistency and Standards: All sports reports follow the same layout, making it easy for fans to quickly find the "Read More" link.

Top 2 Weaknesses

  • Efficiency of Use: While it is easy to see recent sports news, there is no way to filter specifically for one sport (like "Softball only") within the search results.
  • Visibility of System Status: The archives list shows the total number of posts, but it does not specify how many of those are sports-related versus other news.

PART 2 - Comparison and Suggestions

In comparing my analysis with the usability test, both findings highlighted a gap between browsing and searching. While the volunteer enjoyed the visual "Sports" tags and action photos, they found it frustrating to locate a specific game score from October 2021 using only the sidebar archives. My technical analysis confirmed that the system is optimized for "current news" but lacks the "efficiency of use" required for users looking for historical athletic records.

Suggestions for Improvement: I suggest implementing a "Filter by Category" dropdown within the search results to allow users to separate sports news from general campus news instantly.
